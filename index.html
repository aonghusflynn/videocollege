The Video College (formerly North Kensington Video/Drama Project) was a West London charity set up through a local residents' association in 1994. We worked with people traditionally under-represented in film and television industries with a focus on:

  • Young people at risk
  • Those who were missing out, or had missed out, on mainstream education
  • Adults who were unemployed or on low incomes
  • Women in non-traditional roles
  • All local communities, particularly minority communities

What We Did

We provided free, high-quality, production-based video training with a strong drama element. Our programme offered progression routes so that young people and adults could develop both digital video skills and the core skills they needed to get on at school or to move into work, further education or training.

We gave young people and trainees hands-on industry standard training, helping to generate a pool of new entrants who were culturally diverse, often multi-lingual, which was increasingly important for an expanding global market.

Industry Standards

We offered production-based training that introduced industry practice, accredited in line with Occupational Standards. We operated from youth and training studios in North Kensington, where we used industry standard shooting and editing equipment.

Our core team had substantial experience of education and training, career development and industry placements, the arts, youth and community work, and we drew upon a pool of tutors, industry professionals with a minimum of 15 years’ experience.
