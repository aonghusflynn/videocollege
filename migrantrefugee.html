PARTNERSHIP PROJECTS WITH MIGRANT & REFUGEE COMMUNITIES

“I like working in a group, being able to work on our own ideas, in our own language, for our own community”


This area of our work focuses on building partnerships with local migrant and refugee organisations and creating wider opportunities for young people from minority communities. Students have created videos that portray the experience of growing up in London and sometimes the misunderstandings between generations and communities.

We’ve also made a number of videos in Arabic for organisations that want to use video in different languages to reach members of communities, particularly women, who aren’t taking up local support services. Find out more about our work with Community, Voluntary & Statutory Organisations

It's For Us To Decide

Created and performed in Arabic by members of the local Sudanese community the video highlighted the risks of Female Genital Mutilation (FGM). The production was funded by the local Health Authority as it was concerned that practice was still continuing and that women who were affected were not receiving adequate medical care, particularly at marriage and during pregnancy and childbirth. The video was used in the community, for training of health professionals and screened at a conference of FORWARD which works internationally to end FGM. There is now a specialist unit at St. Mary's Paddington.
