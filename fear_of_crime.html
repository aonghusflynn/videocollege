FEAR OF CRIME AND CRIME PREVENTION

When we opened our doors in 1994, fear of crime was a major pre-occupation of the young people we worked with.

STREETLIFE; FEAR OF CRIME; THE CHASE

”There’s a lot more to me than guns and knives and gangs"

In 2006, a film production project with 16/17 year olds on West London ISSP (Intensive Supervision and Surveillance Programme) drew together young people from three different boroughs. The group examined the culture of gun and knife crime and, together with members of Sixty Plus (a voluntary organisation working with older people), reflected on differing perceptions of crime and ways to make communities feel safer.

We again worked with 16 - 18 year olds on West London ISSP on the successful First Light Movies-funded Round and Round

JASON’S CHOICE

In 2010, with funding for Awards for All we ran a programme that arose from a session with 14-16 year olds at the local Golborne YC at which they discussed incidents that had affected them and their friends: how they found ways of avoiding conflict; the fear they felt in the streets; and their unwilling, tangential involvement simply because they were members of the extended family of those participating in violent activities. They wanted a film that gave others a greater understanding of the threat they felt they lived under.

We teamed up with Pathways to Progress which worked with young people who were at risk of being caught up in drug-dealing, ‘post code’ territorialism and continuing violence, including the use of guns.
