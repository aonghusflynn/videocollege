OUT OF SCHOOL LEARNING PROJECTS

 

“We worked in a team to produce our own documentary films…It was fun…. I learnt so much from the tutors”

12-19 year olds can take part in after school sessions to introduce them to the The Video College and new creative, technical and production skills. Young people work in small groups to produce short videos and are encouraged to come up with ideas for larger productions.

After school, at weekends, half term and in holidays, specific times can be set aside for in-depth training with experienced tutors to help young people produce a range of videos in various disciplines, that can then be exhibited at screenings.

The Programme Offers Young People:

:: A secure and structured educational environment which supported their learning and personal development.
:: An opportunity to work in small, confidence building groups.
:: The chance to develop core skills, such as language and literacy, organisation, teamwork, time management and decision-making.
:: The opportunity to create videos that reflect their own interests and ideas and explore personal and social issues.
:: An introduction to industry practice.
:: Opportunities for public recognition, through screenings of work.

Round and Round

One of the UK Film Council First Light films in 2009, Round and Round, made by 16-18 year olds from West London was nominated for a Best Screenplay award, sponsored by EON Productions.  The three nominations in this category were judged by Joseph Fiennes (Shakespeare In Love), director Joanna Hogg (Unrelated), and writer Roger Goldby (The Waiting Room).  The Activision First Light Awards 2009 ceremony, hosted by T4's Miquita Oliver and Rick Edwards took place at the Odeon, Leicester Square, London on Tuesday 17 March 2009.
