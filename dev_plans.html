DEVELOPMENT PLANS

From the mid 90's into the early 2000's The Video College successfully drew down substantial funding from a range of sources in order to continually develop and deliver projects for young people and production-based training that prepared young adults from its target groups for work in the industry. With the downturn, less available resources and changes in policy of funders, government departments and in the profile of arts and film organisations, it became increasingly hard for smaller, community-based charities, such as The Video College, to continue delivery at the level and depth of the previous 15 years.

The Video College neccessarily had to become leaner, losing valued staff and reducing the number of premises, but remained committed to the values and objectives of the organisation. In November we left our office and studio and took the leap of becoming 'mobile' and continuing our focus on working with those most, and increasingly, in need.

We are working on exciting new projects with the community centre which supported us in our early days and with our neighbours, colleagues and friends in local Muslim communities.

We shall be updating the website with films and news of how the projects are developing.
