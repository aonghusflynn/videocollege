PARTNERSHIP PROJECTS WITH MIGRANT & REFUGEE COMMUNITIES

It's For Us To Decide from The Video College on Vimeo.

 It's For Us To Decide (15' 18") Created and performed in Arabic by members of the local Sudanese community the video highlighted the risks of Female Genital Mutilation (FGM). Shot and edited on U-Matic. The video was used in the community, for training of health professionals and screened at a conference of FORWARD, which works internationally to end FGM. There is now a specialist unit at St. Mary's Paddington.

SAFE

A film in Moroccan Arabic about drug misuse and the need for parental support for young people in the Moroccan community. 1999/2000 A partnership project with The Space KC, a health information project, and the Moroccan Information and Advice Centre (MIAC)

WELLBEING IN CHILDBIRTH

With funding from Kensington Housing Trust, the Primary Care Trust and Sure Start, the film was commissioned by the Mental Health Trust, with a steering group of Moroccan, Sudanese, Somali and Iraqi organisations. The film (in Arabic) was aimed at Arabic-speaking women, who were experiencing peri-natal depression, to encourage take up of local support services. It was screened at the Muslim Cultural Heritage Centre.

BETWEEN TWO CULTURES

Funded by the Arts Council’s Arts for Everyone (A4E) scheme, ‘Between Two Cultures’ involved younger and older members of the Filipino community in a training-based documentary production,co-ordinated by an experienced documentary filmmaker. The filming of interviews and cultural events document the experience of Filipino people in this country.
