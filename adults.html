PRODUCTION-BASED TRAINING PROGRAMMES WITH ADULTS FROM OUR TARGET GROUPS

With this strand we want to highlight the experiences and concerns of under-represented communities. Alongside working with young people and adults on industry courses, we also work on projects where older people, 50+, and adults with learning disabilities, gain video skills and produce new work.

Young Mothers Speak Out

 

Why Me?, (19'51") their final production, was made by local mothers on a year-long production-based documentary course.
Funded by The Art's Council's Lottery Scheme, Arts For Everyone, Why Me? was screened at the Curzon, Soho in June 2000 to an audience that included industry producers and a commissioning editor from Channel 4.

None of the participants had had the chance to take part in education and training for some years. We were able to help with locating and organising suitable childcare and paying childcare costs.

"The big plus - the help with childcare and travel. Without it, I'd never have had this opportunity. It's manageable, 2 days out of the house."

Forward From Fifty

Members of Open Age produced a documentary, "Forward From Fifty". Made by a group committed to learning new video and technical skills, it explored attitudes to retirement and the advantages it can bring.

"It has given me an incentive - I thought 'this is great working behind the camera'. And learning how to edit was interesting. I'd like to do more if I had the chance" 58 year old woman

"I looked forward to every moment. The tutors were good teachers, it gave me such confidence.They let us do things for ourselves and when we made mistakes, gently got us back on course again. It has been a most enjoyable time of learning - I've done something I would never have thought possible" 61 year old woman

"It covered most of my requirements for filming with my own camera" 81 year old man

The most memorable times were our shared experiences, the final editing session and the subsequent viewing in a cinema setting" 74 year old woman

“The rate of learning among these students has far exceeded our expectations”

A partnership with Ealing Social Firms Development Project, through which people with physical and learning disabilities joined yearlong, OCN-accredited courses. Students produced ads, a music video, dramas and "Spirit of Life - Karma Attitudes", a documentary from a young Sikh man with cerebral palsy who investigated his religion's attitude to disability.
