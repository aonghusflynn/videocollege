PRODUCTIONS WITH LOCAL ORGANISATIONS

THE BEST CENTRE IN TOWN

Smooth Guide to Golborne: Film Guide to Local Services

From 2001 - 2003, the film guide highlighted the services in our local ward, Golborne - Supplementary Schools, Swinbrook Nursery, Sixty Plus and the Venture Community Association.

Swinbrook Nursery

With support from the Director of Community Production the videos were shot and edited on AVID by ex-trainees.

An elderly resident enjoyed the screening at the Venture Centre:

"because you can't see what's going on in the area or maybe just notice the bad things and it was very nice to see all the good activities that are going on for people"

It's For Us To Decide

Created and performed in Arabic by members of the local Sudanese community the video highlighted the risks of Female Genital Mutilation (FGM). The production was funded by the local Health Authority as it was concerned that practice was still continuing and that women who were affected were not receiving adequate medical care, particularly at marriage and during pregnancy and childbirth. The video was used in the community, for training of health professionals and screened at a conference of FORWARD which works internationally to end FGM. There is now a specialist unit at St. Mary's Paddington.

 

Like Being Rich

Educational film for Kensington & Chelsea's and Hampshire's Social Services Departments.

The film was used by the Council's Children's Resource Teams which work with young people living in care. It is aimed at teachers, including trainee teachers, carers and parents and, as part of a Government initiative, highlights the importance of education for young people in care and the barriers and difficulties which they may face.

Britain in Bloom

A film to support the Royal Borough of Kensington & Chelsea's entry in the Britain In Bloom competition.

A Day in The Life

A film following the lives of three local residents with disabilities as part of the Royal Borough of Kensington & Chelsea's consultation programme with community partners, including Action Disability Kensington & Chelsea.
