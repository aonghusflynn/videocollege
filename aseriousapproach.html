A Serious Approach

The Video College originated from the Residents’ Association of the Wornington Green Estate, West London, which was involved in the regeneration of the estate. A survey undertaken in the early 1990s revealed a ‘serious need for supervised, structured activities for young people’ in an area of high-density housing and rising crime.

What was, and remains, distinctive about our work was our commitment to both supporting individuals in the local community and offering progression opportunities into the highly competitive world of television and film. In so doing, we were able to work with, and draw on the experience and advice of, not only community and statutory organisations, but also broadcasters and film industry professionals.

We recognised that the industry workforce failed to reflect the make-up of the UK population. We always believed there was a wealth of untapped creative talent in London and our purpose was to encourage people of all ages to discover and develop their skills in digital (and initially analogue) video. Our emphasis was on students of all ages gaining hands-on technical and creative training in the context of production. From the outset they were introduced to the protocols and practice of professional production, the planning and paperwork, and the dedication and work ethic needed to bring fresh, original stories based on their own experiences to the screen.
